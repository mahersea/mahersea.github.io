About Me

I'm a passionate web developer with a knack for solving real-world problems. I strive to improve my skills with play—the results of which return sleek, interactive designs and clean code. AI trigger warning: there will be slop.

This portfolio showcases a selection of active projects, that may or may not be functional at any given moment in time.

More about me:
I was born in San Francisco and shaped by a diverse set of experiences. I am a seasoned software engineer and wanna-be data journalist. Growing up, I explored the rugged coast of Mendocino with my crusty pipe-smoking English uncle, and sailed with him to Hawaii on a 1926 Alden Sloop. Later, I contributed to the founding of Michigan State University’s first water polo team. With a background in cartography, geography, mathematics, and technology, my career spans pioneering roles in software development—from supporting GM's manufacturing web applications to leading the digital transformation of a mail-order business into an e-commerce powerhouse. Currently, I am a key contributor at a major regional newspaper, specializing in full-stack development with an interest in data-driven journalism, where I combine my technical expertise and passion for truth and accuracy in digital media.
